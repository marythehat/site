About Dr. Z

The mind behind tomorrow’s meals.

Dr. Oscar Zephestus, known publicly as Dr. Z, was born in 1979 in the small town of St. Joseph, Missouri. Quiet and withdrawn as a child, he spent most of his time alone—disassembling appliances, sketching impossible machines, and imagining systems that worked better than the ones around him.

His constant tinkering eventually worried his mother, who struck a compromise: no inventing until dinner was made. What began as a boundary quickly became a revelation. Cooking, Dr. Z discovered, was simply another system—one plagued by inconsistency, waste, and human error.

As his fascination with food grew, so did his ambition. Dr. Z began applying engineering principles to the kitchen, convinced that meals could be optimized the same way machines were. Precision, repeatability, and efficiency became his guiding philosophy.

“Why cook,” Dr. Z later asked, “when perfection can be automated?”
